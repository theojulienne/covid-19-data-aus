COVID-19 update – 10 November 2020

The Department of Health has reported two new cases of COVID-19 in Western Australia overnight.

Both cases are males, one in their 50s and one in their 60s, who have recently undertaken international travel. One is a maritime worker, who flew in to join a vessel. Both cases are in hotel quarantine.

The State’s positive COVID-19 case total stands at 778. WA Health is monitoring 12 active cases.

To date 757 confirmed cases have recovered from the virus.

Yesterday 632 people presented to WA COVID-19 clinics – 623 were assessed and 622 were swabbed.

There have been 508,425 COVID-19 tests performed in WA. Of those tested, 85,193 were from regional WA.

Visit WA Health’s HealthyWA website for the latest information on COVID-19.
