COVID-19 update 25 February 2021

The Department of Health has reported no new cases of COVID-19 overnight.

The State’s total number of confirmed cases stands at 913.

WA Health is monitoring three active cases of COVID-19 and 901 people have recovered from the virus in WA.

To date, 24 cases of variant strains have been detected in Western Australia (17 B.1.1.7 strain and 7 B.1.351 strain). Two remain active and are in hotel quarantine.

Yesterday 415 people were swabbed at WA Health COVID clinics.

Visit WA Health’s HealthyWA website for the latest information on COVID-19. 

Follow us on Twitter: @WAHealth

Note: The cases of variant strains reported above are not new cases. Genome sequencing – to define the strain of the virus – is a separate laboratory process carried out after someone who tests positive to COVID-19. All cases, regardless of strain, are managed by WA’s public health experts. The strains previously reported as the UK variant strain and South African variant strain will now be referred to as B.1.1.7 and B.1.351 respectively.

The total number of metropolitan and regional tests is not available today due to collating delays – total figures will be updated on Friday, 26 February 2021.
